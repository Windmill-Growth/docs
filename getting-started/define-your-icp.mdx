---
title: "Define your ICP"
description: "Use ICP criteria to decide which people and companies count as qualified leads in traxy."
---

Your ICP decides who counts as a qualified lead in `traxy`. A narrow ICP usually improves precision. A broader ICP usually increases volume. Revisit it whenever your target market changes.

## Where to update your ICP

- During onboarding, before you activate your workspace.
- Later in **ICP Configuration** from the main app navigation.

## People criteria

Use people criteria to describe the individual you want `traxy` to prioritize.

- **Job Titles** help define seniority or function.
- **Departments** help narrow the team you want to reach.
- **Locations** help limit results to the regions that matter to you.

## Company criteria

Use company criteria to describe the type of company you want to target.

- **Company Size**
- **Industries**
- **Revenue**
- **Funding**
- **Keywords & Niches**

These fields work together. For example, broad industries with narrow keywords can be more useful than a long list of overlapping industries.

## Build a better ICP

1. Start with the fewest signals that clearly define a fit.
2. Use terms your buyers would use about themselves or their companies.
3. Remove broad terms if low-fit leads keep appearing.
4. Broaden one field at a time if you are seeing engagement but not enough qualified leads.

<Tip>
  Save changes, watch your dashboard, leads, and post-level results for a short period, then adjust one variable at a time. That makes it easier to see what changed lead quality.
</Tip>

## When to update your ICP

Update your ICP when:

- your team changes who it wants to target
- you are getting too many low-fit leads
- you are seeing real engagement but very few qualified leads
- you launch into a new market, region, or segment
