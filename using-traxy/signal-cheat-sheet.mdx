Use this page as a fast reference when you know a lead engaged but you need to decide what to do next.

## How to use this sheet

Start with three questions:

1. How strong is the ICP fit?
2. How fresh is the signal?
3. Does the signal show real interest or just light awareness?

Then use the table below to choose the first move.

| Signal | What it usually means | Best first move | Channel | Timing | Example opener |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Comment on your post or a relevant post | Active interest and clear topic relevance | Reach out directly with one tight angle | LinkedIn or email | Same day or next business day | Your comment on [topic] stood out to me. |
| Share or repost | Stronger endorsement than a like | Thank them, then bridge to the problem or takeaway | LinkedIn first | Same day or next business day | Saw you shared [topic]. It seems especially relevant for [their team type]. |
| Repeat engagement across multiple posts | Ongoing interest, not a one-off | Reference the broader theme, not every action | Email or LinkedIn | Within 48 hours of the most recent signal | You have been engaging with a few conversations around [theme], so I thought I would reach out directly. |
| Multiple people from one company engage | Potential account-level interest | Coordinate a multi-threaded approach | Email for senior contacts, LinkedIn for lighter touches | Within 48 hours | A few people from [company] have been engaging with [topic], so I thought I would reach out with one practical angle. |
| Single like or light reaction | Light awareness, weak standalone signal | Work only if ICP fit is strong | Usually LinkedIn | Within 48 hours if you work it at all | It looks like [topic] may be relevant to your team right now. |
| Old signal older than 14 days | Context, but no longer urgency | Treat like cold outbound with context | Email or LinkedIn | When capacity allows | I know this is not brand new, but [topic] still looked relevant enough to reach out. |
| Event session post or recap | Topic interest tied to a time-bound moment | Reference the exact session or takeaway | LinkedIn first, then email | During the event or within 48 hours after | Saw your post about [session topic] at [event]. |
| Thoughtful comment on your own content | Warmest content-led signal | Reply publicly first, then follow up privately | LinkedIn | Public reply within 24 hours, DM soon after | Really appreciated your take on [topic]. |

## Fast rules

- Comments, shares, repeat engagement, and company clusters beat one-off reactions.
- If the engagement was light, reference the topic instead of the exact action.
- If the signal is old, make the topic do the work. Do not lean on the stale action.
- If you cannot explain why the signal matters in one sentence, do not reach out yet.

## What to skip

Skip or hold when:

- fit is weak and the signal is weak
- the person was already contacted recently
- the engagement came from a noisy, high-volume post with no follow-through
- the topic is sensitive enough that outreach would feel opportunistic
