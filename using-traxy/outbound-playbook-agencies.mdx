Use this playbook when you sell agency or consulting services and want your outreach to demonstrate judgment, not just availability.

## What is different for agencies

Agency buying is more relationship-driven than product-led. Prospects are often evaluating your thinking before they evaluate your offer.

That means the first touch should prove you understand the problem, not just announce that your agency exists.

## Signals that matter most

Prioritize in this order:

1. Engagement on a problem your agency solves
2. Public frustration with a current approach or result
3. Engagement with your case studies or thought leadership
4. Hiring activity that points to a capability gap your agency could support
5. Shared engagement paths that could create a warm introduction

## Best channel and timing

LinkedIn is often the best first channel for agencies because it feels more like a peer conversation.

Email works well when:

- the signal is strong
- you have a relevant case study
- you want to share a sharper observation with more context

Move within 48 hours for content engagement signals. Event or hiring-based signals can sometimes stay workable a bit longer, but freshness still helps.

## Messaging angle

Lead with a useful observation or short insight.

Good agency angles:

- a quick read on what is likely going wrong
- a relevant example from a similar company
- a concrete offer to share a teardown, resource, or short case study

## Suggested sequence

1. Day 0: LinkedIn note or email based on signal strength
2. Day 3: follow-up with a useful example, teardown, or case study
3. Day 7: one final note with a softer interest-based ask

## Example LinkedIn message

```text
Hey [Name], your take on [topic] resonated.

We have been seeing the same pattern with [their company type] teams lately.

We just worked through this with a similar company. Happy to share what helped if useful.
```

## Example email

```text
Subject: [topic]

[First name] - your post on [topic] caught my eye.

The issue you pointed to is usually where [their company type] teams start to lose momentum.

If useful, I can send over a short example of how another team tackled it.
```

## Common mistakes

- leading with credentials instead of the prospect’s problem
- asking for a call before demonstrating value
- treating every company with a visible problem as a good fit
- describing your agency so vaguely that the buyer still does not know what you actually help with

## What good looks like

- the prospect feels understood before they feel sold
- your first touch gives value or framing, not just a request
- selectivity improves because you focus on clear problem signals, not generic awareness
