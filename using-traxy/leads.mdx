The leads area is where you review, classify, enrich, and route the people `traxy` identified from LinkedIn engagement.

<Frame>
  ![Leadsv4](/images/Leadsv4.png)
</Frame>

## Use the main tabs

`traxy` separates leads into four main views:

- **Active** for leads you are working now
- **Clients** for people you already closed or want to treat as customers
- **Snoozed** for leads you want to pause
- **Bad fit** for people you do not want in your active review flow

## Search and filter leads

Use the toolbar to narrow the list quickly.

The main filters include:

- search
- ICP match
- intent
- relationship strength
- owner
- monitored profile
- engaged with
- post source

Open **Advanced Filters** when you need more control. Advanced filters include:

- **People Criteria** with **Job Titles**, **Departments**, and **Location**
- **Lead Criteria** with **Engaged in Last 24 Hours** and **First Seen**
- **Company Criteria** with **Company Name**, **Industries**, **Company Size**, and **Keywords & Niches**

If your workspace tracks more than one profile, use the selector at the top of **Leads** before you filter. That keeps counts and results aligned to the profile scope you want to review.

## Work inside the table

You can sort the table, change visible columns, and open a full lead detail view from the list.

Use **Edit Columns** when your team wants a tighter working view for qualification, enrichment, or routing.

Useful fields include:

- **Last engagement** to see the most recent like, comment, or repost
- delivery status columns when you use Slack or Webhook
- contact fields after enrichment

The lead detail view is the best place to:

- review engagement history
- inspect the qualification summary
- review fit feedback and notes
- enrich email or phone
- send an individual lead to Slack or Webhook

## Review and move leads quickly

You can review leads directly from the table without opening each record.

Use quick actions or the detail panel to:

- mark a lead as a good fit
- mark a lead as a bad fit
- mark a lead as a client
- snooze a lead
- undo a recent classification change

You can also apply these actions in bulk after you select multiple leads.

## Use Auto-Enrich

Owners and admins can manage **Auto-Enrich** from the leads page.

When you turn it on, `traxy` can automatically enrich newly qualified leads that meet your minimum ICP score.

You can choose whether `traxy` looks for:

- email
- phone
- both

Credits are charged only when contact data is found.

Auto-Enrich does not backfill older posts. Use [Post Signals](/using-traxy/content-calendar) when you want to find leads from older engagement.

## Manage company exclusions

From a company's hover card in the leads table, you can add that company to your **Blacklist**.

When you do that, future leads from that company stop appearing in your workspace.

You can manage blocked domains later in **Settings** \> **Blacklist**.

## Send and route leads

Depending on your workspace setup, you can:

- snooze or restore leads
- export selected leads
- enrich leads
- send leads to Slack or Webhook

If you want automatic delivery, connect your workspace in [Integrations](/using-traxy/integrations). If you prefer more control, send leads manually from the leads table after review.

Automatic delivery only applies to leads that qualify after auto-send is enabled. It does not backfill older leads that were already in your workspace.

## Understand locked or paused lead states

If your workspace runs out of available qualification credits, `traxy` can pause new qualification work.

When that happens:

- some new leads may stay locked or hidden
- qualification actions may stop until credits reset
- admins may need to upgrade, raise the monthly budget, or turn on [Pay-as-you-go](/using-traxy/usage-based-credits)

<Info>
  Credits can affect qualification and enrichment actions. If an action is unavailable, check [Billing & Plans](/using-traxy/billing-and-credits).
</Info>

If you want a practical outreach workflow after `traxy` surfaces a lead, use [outbound to signal-based leads](/using-traxy/outbound-to-signal-based-leads).
