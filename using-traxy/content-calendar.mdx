Use Post Signals when you want to work from a searchable post feed instead of jumping between the dashboard and leads.

## Change profile scope

If your workspace tracks more than one LinkedIn profile, use the profile selector at the top of Post Signals before you review posts or qualify a post.

This keeps counts, credit previews, and qualified lead totals aligned to the profile scope you are reviewing.

## Use the summary cards

Post Signals shows four summary cards for the selected time range:

- **Qualified Posts**
- **Total Leads from Posts**
- **Avg ICP Match**
- **Top Performing Account**

Use them to spot which posts and profiles are producing the strongest lead signal.

## Search and filter posts

Use the toolbar to work from the post feed.

You can:

- search post text
- switch between **List** and **Grid**
- sort by date, leads, average ICP match, or engagement
- change the time range to **Last 7 days**, **Last 30 days**, **Last 90 days**, or **All time**
- filter by qualification status, **Auto-Scan**, and minimum average ICP

If no results appear, remove filters or widen the time range before you assume post data is missing.

## Find leads from a tracked post

When a post has engagement, you can run **Find Leads** directly from Post Signals.

This is useful when:

- you want to review one post at a time
- you want to prioritize a post with strong buyer signals
- you want to refresh lead discovery after a high-performing post

Before you confirm, `traxy` starts with an estimated credit range for that action.

Use **Get Exact Credits** when you want the exact count before you continue.

The estimate can end up lower in practice because people `traxy` already qualified in the last 30 days are excluded from billing.

Only posts with engagement can be used to find leads. The action can stay blocked if credits are unavailable, Pay-as-you-go is off, or the workspace monthly budget cap has been reached.

The credit preview reflects your current profile scope.

## Qualify a post by URL

Use **Qualify Post** at the top of Post Signals when the post is not already in your feed.

1. Click **Qualify Post**.
2. Paste the LinkedIn post URL.
3. Review the resolved post preview.
4. Choose the relationship for that profile if needed.
5. Review the credit estimate or get exact credits.
6. Click **Find Leads**.

This is useful when you want to qualify a specific post right away instead of waiting for it to show up in the feed.

## Qualify multiple posts

In list view, select more than one post and use **Qualify Posts**.

The batch dialog lets you:

- review the selected posts
- see total engagement across the selection
- remove posts you do not want to run yet
- estimate or calculate exact credits before you submit

## Use Auto-Scan

Turn on **Auto-Scan** for a post when you want `traxy` to keep qualifying new engagement on that post as it comes in.

By default, only your last 5 posts are actively monitored. Enabling **Auto-Scan** keeps a post qualifying indefinitely.

## Review post analytics

Open post analytics to see the qualified leads tied to a specific post. From there, you can jump back into the leads view to continue working those people.

Post analytics is useful when you want to:

- see which specific post produced the lead
- compare high-performing posts by lead quality
- move from a post into a filtered leads view

<Tip>
  If a post shows strong engagement but weak average ICP match, that usually signals an ICP or threshold adjustment opportunity.
</Tip>
