Use Post Signals when you want to inspect performance one post at a time instead of working from the full leads list.

## Change profile scope

If your workspace tracks more than one LinkedIn profile, use the profile selector at the top of Post Signals before you review a month or qualify a post.

This keeps the calendar, credit preview, and qualified lead counts aligned to the profile scope you are reviewing.

## Use the month view

Post Signals shows your posts by month and lets you move backward or forward across available months.

<Frame>
  ![Screenshot2026 03 16at11 30 00PM](/images/Screenshot2026-03-16at11.30.00PM.png)
</Frame>

Use it to:

- review posting activity by day
- compare post performance over time
- inspect engagement before you run **Find Leads**

If a day has more than one post, open that day to review each post separately.

## Track performance

<Frame>
  ![Postlikes 1](/images/postlikes-1.png)
</Frame>

The performance area helps you compare monthly activity. Depending on the view, you can review:

- posts
- likes
- comments
- reposts

## Find leads from a post

When a post has engagement, you can run **Find Leads** directly from Post Signals.

This is useful when:

- you want to review one post at a time
- you want to prioritize a post with strong buyer signals
- you want to refresh lead discovery after a high-performing post

Before you confirm, `traxy` previews the credit use for that action. Only posts with engagement can be used to find leads, and the action can be blocked if credits are unavailable.

The credit preview reflects your current profile scope.

## Review post analytics

Open post analytics to see the qualified leads tied to a specific post. From there, you can jump back into the leads view to continue working those people.

Post analytics is useful when you want to:

- see which specific post produced the lead
- compare high-performing posts by lead quality
- move from a post into a filtered leads view
