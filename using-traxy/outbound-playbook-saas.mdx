Use this playbook when you sell software and want to turn engagement signals into stronger outbound.

## What is different in SaaS

SaaS outbound is crowded. Buyers get too much generic outreach, and buying decisions often involve multiple stakeholders.

That makes signal quality more valuable. When a lead engages with a problem your product solves, you can be specific without guessing.

## Signals that matter most

Prioritize in this order:

1. Comments on a problem your product solves
2. Multiple people from the same account engaging with related topics
3. Repeat engagement from a decision-maker
4. Engagement around competitor or comparison content
5. Fresh role changes for strong-fit leaders, especially when paired with topic engagement

## Best channel and timing

Use email first when the ICP fit and signal are both strong.

Use LinkedIn first when:

- the signal is lighter
- the engagement happened on LinkedIn
- you want a lower-friction opener before sending a fuller business case

Move within 24 to 48 hours while the topic is still current.

## Messaging angle

Lead with the problem they engaged with, not your product.

Good angles:

- a problem pattern you keep seeing in similar teams
- a short operational observation
- a practical takeaway that makes the issue easier to frame internally

## Multi-thread the right way

If multiple people from one company engage, do not send the same note to everyone.

- send the most senior contact a concise email tied to business impact
- send a lighter LinkedIn note to a more junior or hands-on contact
- keep the topic consistent, but adapt the angle to the role

## Suggested sequence

1. Day 0: email to the primary contact
2. Day 2 or 3: LinkedIn touch for the same contact or a second stakeholder
3. Day 5: follow-up email with a different angle or short example
4. Day 8 or 9: short close-the-loop follow-up

## Example email

```text
Subject: [topic]

Hey [Name],

Saw you were engaging with [topic].

We hear that exact issue from [their company type] teams a lot, especially when [specific nuance].

Happy to send over the short version of what seems to work if useful.
```

## Common mistakes

- treating every like as a buying signal
- sending the same message to every stakeholder
- pitching features instead of the problem they engaged with
- ignoring account-level patterns when several people from one company are active

## What good looks like

- your best signals come from comments, repeat engagement, and company clusters
- hot signals outperform stale ones
- multi-threaded accounts outperform single-threaded outreach when the account fit is strong
